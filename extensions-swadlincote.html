Moving home can be expensive and disruptive, especially when you already like your street, the local schools, and the surrounding area. A well-designed extension gives you the opportunity to stay where you are while creating space that genuinely supports the way your household lives now.

At AB Construction UK Ltd, we build extensions that are tailored to the property, the budget, and the long-term goals of the homeowner. Some clients want a single-storey rear extension to open up the kitchen and create a brighter family room. Others need a side extension, a wraparound extension, or a two-storey addition that delivers more bedrooms, a larger bathroom, or a separate utility space. Properties around Swadlincote and South Derbyshire vary from traditional brick-built terraces and post-war semis to detached family homes and newer developments. We take a practical view from the outset, helping clients think about structural alterations, insulation, natural light, building regulations, and the details that influence how the finished room will actually feel to live in every day.

A successful extension project is never just about adding square metres. It is about improving circulation through the house, making better use of existing rooms, and delivering value that lasts. We can help homeowners create open-plan kitchen dining spaces, family rooms, home offices, downstairs shower rooms, utility areas, enlarged lounges, and smarter transitions into the garden. Because we also provide brickwork, joinery, kitchen fitting, bathroom work, and renovation services, we can coordinate the whole package with one dependable local team.

Why homeowners in Swadlincote choose an extension

For many households, an extension is the most sensible way to create space without giving up a location they already enjoy. In Swadlincote, Woodville, Newhall, and nearby villages, families often want to remain close to schools, work routes, relatives, and local amenities. Extending can be more cost-effective than moving once you consider estate agency fees, stamp duty, legal costs, removals, and the risk of buying a property that still requires updating.

Extensions also let you solve very specific layout problems. A cramped kitchen can become the social centre of the home. A dark rear room can be opened up with wider glazing. An underused side return can become valuable living space. Instead of adapting your lifestyle around a restrictive floor plan, the building can be adapted around the way you actually live.

Extension types we commonly build

Our projects include rear extensions, side extensions, wraparound extensions, kitchen extensions, utility room additions, and two-storey schemes. The right option depends on the shape of your plot, the existing layout, and what you want the extra space to achieve. A small rear extension can completely change the feel of a house when combined with internal knock-through work, while a larger two-storey extension can transform both the ground floor and the first floor at the same time.

We discuss practicality as well as appearance. That means considering how steelwork may be required, how new and existing floors align, where drainage runs, whether roof lights are appropriate, and how to maintain a natural flow between the original property and the new space.

What our extension process looks like

Every project starts with a conversation about priorities. We listen to how the current home works, what is frustrating about it, how many people use the space, and what the budget needs to achieve. From there, we help shape the brief, identify buildability issues, and explain the likely stages of work so there are no surprises later.

Once the design and approvals are in place, construction usually moves through site preparation, foundations, drainage, structural openings, brickwork, roofing, first-fix services, plastering, second-fix carpentry, and final finishing. Throughout the build, we focus on keeping the site organised and the client informed. Good communication matters just as much as good workmanship on an occupied residential project.

Choosing a local extension builder gives you an advantage because experience with the local housing stock makes problem-solving faster and more realistic. Older homes in South Derbyshire can hide irregular walls, outdated electrics, and drainage arrangements that only become visible once work begins. A builder who regularly works in the area is more likely to anticipate those issues and budget sensibly from the beginning.

Local knowledge also helps when coordinating trades, sourcing matching materials, and planning the sequence of work to reduce disruption. We aim to deliver extensions that look right from the street, function properly in daily use, and add lasting value to the property.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need planning permission for a house extension in Swadlincote?

Some extensions fall within permitted development, but larger, taller, or more complex schemes may need formal planning permission. We always recommend confirming the exact position for your property before work starts.

How long does a typical extension take to build?

The construction period depends on size, structural complexity, specification, and weather, but many residential extension projects take several weeks to a few months once work begins on site.

Can you handle the full build and internal finish?

Yes. We can manage the structural shell, brickwork, joinery, kitchen or bathroom fitting, plastering, and finishing elements needed to turn the new space into a usable part of the home.
