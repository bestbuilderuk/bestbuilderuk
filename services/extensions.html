Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need planning permission for a house extension in Swadlincote?

Many house extensions in Swadlincote fall under Permitted Development Rights and do not require formal planning permission, provided they meet specific criteria regarding size, height, and proximity to boundaries. However, larger extensions, properties in conservation areas, or listed buildings will require planning permission from South Derbyshire District Council. AB Construction UK Ltd provides free guidance on planning requirements for your specific project.

How much does a home extension cost per square metre?

In 2026, home extension costs in South Derbyshire typically range from £1,800 to £2,500 per square metre for a standard single-storey extension, depending on the specification and finish. Double-storey extensions offer better value per square metre as they share the same foundations and roof structure. Contact AB Construction UK Ltd on 01283 310115 for a free, detailed quote.

How long does a typical house extension take to build?

A single-storey rear extension typically takes 10–14 weeks from groundworks to completion, while a double-storey extension can take 14–22 weeks. Timelines depend on size, complexity, weather conditions, and any planning permission requirements. We provide a detailed project schedule before work begins.

Can you manage the entire extension process from design to completion?

Yes. AB Construction UK Ltd provides a complete, fully managed service for all house extensions in Swadlincote and South Derbyshire. We coordinate everything from initial design concepts and planning applications through to the final decoration and handover, managing all trades in-house.

Will a house extension add value to my property in South Derbyshire?

Yes. A well-designed and professionally built house extension can add 10–25% to the value of your property. A single-storey extension typically adds 5–10%, while a double-storey extension can add 15–25%. The key is quality construction and a design that complements the existing property.
