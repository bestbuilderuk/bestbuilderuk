Home Extensions in Swadlincote: Everything You Need to Know

Published by AB Construction Ltd | February 2026

Home / Blog / Home Extensions in Swadlincote: Everything You Need to Know

Is your family growing? Do you dream of a larger kitchen or an extra living space? If you love your home and its Swadlincote location but need more room, a home extension is the perfect solution. It allows you to create the space you need without the stress and expense of moving.

This guide covers everything you need to know about planning a home extension in Swadlincote.

Types of Home Extensions

There are several types of extensions, and the best one for you will depend on your property, your budget, and your needs.

Rear Extension

The most popular choice, extending out from the back of your house. Perfect for creating a large open-plan kitchen-diner that connects to the garden. This pairs beautifully with a new kitchen installation. See our kitchen renovation ideas for inspiration.

Side-Return Extension

Ideal for terraced or semi-detached homes, this uses the often-neglected alleyway space at the side of the property. It's a brilliant way to widen your existing kitchen or living area.

Wraparound Extension

This combines a rear and side-return extension, creating a huge L-shaped space that can completely transform the ground floor of your home. It requires expert brickwork to ensure a seamless finish.

Double-Storey Extension

A cost-effective way to add space on two levels. You could extend your kitchen and add a new bedroom or bathroom above it, for example. While the groundwork and roofing costs are similar to a single-storey, you get twice the space.

The Extension Process: A Step-by-Step Guide

Building an extension can seem daunting, but a good builder will manage the process for you. Here's a typical timeline:

  1. Initial Consultation and Quote: Discuss your ideas with a builder like AB Construction Ltd. We provide a free, detailed quote.
  2. Design and Drawings: An architect or architectural designer will draw up detailed plans for your extension.
  3. Planning Permission / Permitted Development: Your plans are submitted to South Derbyshire District Council. Many extensions fall under 'Permitted Development' and don't need full planning permission, but they still need to be certified as lawful. Your builder will handle this.
  4. Building Regulations: The detailed plans must be approved by Building Control to ensure they meet safety and construction standards.
  5. Construction: This is the main phase, covering groundwork, brickwork, roofing, fitting windows, electrics, plumbing, and plastering.
  6. Finishing: The final touches, like fitting the kitchen, laying flooring, and decorating. Our joinery team handles all bespoke woodwork.
  7. Completion and Handover: Your beautiful new space is ready for you to enjoy!

Do I Need Planning Permission in South Derbyshire?

Many home extensions in Swadlincote can be built under Permitted Development Rights. These are government rules that allow for certain modifications without needing to apply for full planning permission. For example, a single-storey rear extension can typically project up to 3 metres from the original rear wall of an attached house, or 4 metres for a detached house.

However, the rules are complex and depend on many factors, including whether your property is in a conservation area, whether you've already used your Permitted Development allowance, and the height and proximity to boundaries. It is crucial to get expert advice.

A professional builder like AB Construction will assess your plans and manage the entire approvals process, ensuring your project is fully compliant. This is one of the many benefits of choosing a local builder.
