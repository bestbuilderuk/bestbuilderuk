Do You Need Planning Permission for an Extension in Swadlincote?

Published by AB Construction UK Ltd | April 2026

Home / Blog / Do You Need Planning Permission for an Extension in Swadlincote?

A practical explanation of planning permission and permitted development for homeowners considering an extension in Swadlincote.

The short answer

If you are asking do you need planning permission for an extension in Swadlincote?, the truthful answer is that some homeowners do and some do not. Certain house extensions in England can fall within permitted development rights, which means a full planning application may not be required. However, those rights depend on strict conditions relating to size, height, location, how much of the original plot is covered, and whether the property has already been altered. The details matter enormously.

For homeowners in Swadlincote and the wider South Derbyshire area, it is sensible to treat permitted development as something to verify rather than assume. Even if a project seems modest, factors such as previous extensions, proximity to boundaries, special design constraints, or the exact roof and wall arrangement can affect whether formal permission is needed.

What permitted development usually means

Permitted development rights are national rules that allow some domestic improvement works to go ahead without a full householder planning application, provided the proposal stays within the relevant limits. For extensions, those limits can cover matters such as rear projection, overall height, eaves height, materials, and the relationship to the existing house. Two-storey work, side extensions, and homes with complicated planning history often need especially careful checking.

Even where full planning permission is not required, building regulations approval is usually still relevant. Homeowners therefore need to think about legal compliance in two layers: planning position and building control position.

Why local context still matters

National permitted development rules do not remove the importance of local context. South Derbyshire design guidance still matters when a planning application is needed, and the character of the street, neighbouring amenity, and overall appearance of the extension can all influence the outcome. Some properties may also be affected by conditions or restrictions that limit normal permitted development rights.

That is why homeowners should avoid relying on broad online assumptions. A proposal that works under permitted development on one house may need permission on another house a few streets away because the planning history or site conditions are different.

When homeowners should be cautious

You should be particularly cautious if the extension is two storeys, extends close to a boundary, significantly changes the front or side appearance of the house, or forms part of a broader remodel. It is also wise to check carefully if the property has already been extended before, as previous works can reduce what remains available under permitted development.

Homeowners often find it useful to obtain clear professional advice early rather than plan the entire scheme around an assumption that later proves incorrect. That can save time, redesign costs, and frustration.

A practical next step for Swadlincote homeowners

For most homeowners, the practical next step is to review the proposal against current Planning Portal guidance and, where necessary, check with the local authority or a suitable professional before starting work. South Derbyshire also provides ways for householders to make enquiries about whether planning permission is likely to be required for extensions or alterations.

In simple terms, do not guess. Permitted development can be extremely useful, but only when the proposal is properly understood and checked. A well-planned extension starts with legal clarity as well as design clarity.

Why local, practical advice matters

General guidance can help you ask better questions, but every property is different. The final answer on cost, planning position, or project suitability depends on the exact house, the existing layout, and the level of finish you want. That is why local, property-specific advice is always the best next step once you move from research into action.
