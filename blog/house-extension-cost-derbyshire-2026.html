How Much Does a House Extension Cost in Derbyshire in 2026?

Published by AB Construction UK Ltd | April 2026

Home / Blog / How Much Does a House Extension Cost in Derbyshire in 2026?

House extensions remain one of the most effective ways to create more space without moving, especially for growing families in Swadlincote, Burton upon Trent, and the wider Derbyshire area. In 2026, most homeowners are balancing rising material prices with the long-term value that a well-planned extension can add to a property.

Although every scheme is different, there are clear budget ranges that can help you plan sensibly before you speak to a builder. The figures below reflect common extension types seen across South Derbyshire and should be treated as working estimates rather than fixed quotations.

Typical Extension Budget Ranges in Derbyshire

Extension typeTypical 2026 budgetNotes
Small single-storey rear extension£35,000–£55,000Ideal for opening up kitchens or adding a dining area.
Medium rear or side-return extension£55,000–£85,000Common where structural alterations and new steels are needed.
Wraparound extension£80,000–£130,000+Best for major open-plan family living spaces.
Two-storey extension£95,000–£160,000+Adds the most area, but also increases structural and regulatory complexity.

These figures exclude unusual ground conditions, premium glazing packages, and very high-end kitchens or bespoke joinery.

What Makes One Extension Cost More Than Another?

The biggest price differences usually come from foundations, drainage changes, roof complexity, and the amount of structural steelwork required. Interior specification also has a major effect. A simple shell extension is very different from a turnkey space with underfloor heating, aluminium doors, fitted joinery, and a designer kitchen.

How to Budget Properly

A useful rule is to set aside a contingency of at least 10% for unforeseen work. Older properties often reveal hidden issues once walls are opened up, especially where previous alterations, poor drainage, or outdated electrics are involved.

Choosing the Right Builder

When comparing quotes, homeowners should look beyond the headline price. Ask what is included, whether structural works and building control coordination are covered, and how the builder deals with changes once work begins. The best quote is usually the clearest and most transparent one, not simply the cheapest.
